Spaghetti Recipe

Recipe Description

To make a delicious spaghetti dish, start by boiling a large pot of salted water and cooking your spaghetti according to the package instructions. While the pasta is cooking, heat a tablespoon of olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Sauté chopped onions and minced garlic until they turn golden and fragrant. This step adds a nice depth of flavor to the sauce.

Next, add your choice of ground meat, such as beef, pork, or a mix, to the skillet, breaking it up with a spoon. Cook the meat until browned, then pour in a can of crushed tomatoes. Season the sauce with salt, pepper, and dried Italian herbs like basil and oregano. Let the sauce simmer for 10–15 minutes to blend the flavors, stirring occasionally. If you prefer a bit of spice, add a pinch of red pepper flakes.

Once the pasta is ready, drain it and add it directly to the skillet, tossing it with the sauce to coat evenly. Finish the dish by adding a handful of fresh basil or parsley for a fresh touch. Serve the spaghetti with a generous sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese and a drizzle of olive oil. Enjoy this classic Italian dish with a side of garlic bread or a green salad.

Ingredients

  • 400g spaghetti
  • Salt (for boiling water and seasoning)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 500g ground beef, pork, or a mix
  • 1 can (400g) crushed tomatoes
  • Black pepper, to taste
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Red pepper flakes (optional, to taste)
  • Fresh basil or parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (for serving)

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions.
  2. While the pasta is cooking, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a skillet over medium heat.
  3. Sauté the chopped onion and minced garlic until golden and fragrant.
  4. Add the ground meat (beef, pork, or a mix) to the skillet, breaking it up with a spoon, and cook until browned.
  5. Pour in the can of crushed tomatoes and season with salt, pepper, dried basil, and dried oregano.
  6. If desired, add a pinch of red pepper flakes for a bit of heat.
  7. Let the sauce simmer for 10–15 minutes, stirring occasionally to blend the flavors.
  8. Drain the cooked pasta and add it directly to the skillet with the sauce, tossing to coat evenly.
  9. Garnish with fresh basil or parsley and serve with grated Parmesan cheese.
